# Morning catch-up digest

**Who:** Knowledge worker with multiple communication channels

**Intent:** "I want to open my laptop in the morning and have a single summary of everything that happened overnight — Slack mentions, emails that need replies, calendar for the day, and any tickets that moved. I don't want to open 5 apps and scan each one."

**Context:** Currently takes 15-20 minutes of app-switching every morning. Most of the scanning is wasted — only 3-5 items actually need attention. The rest is noise. The user wants the signal extracted for them before they even start.

**Success:** A digest appears (Slack DM, email, or local file) by the time the user sits down. It's skimmable in under 2 minutes. Items that need action are clearly separated from FYI items. The digest is consistent enough day-to-day that the user develops muscle memory for scanning it.
