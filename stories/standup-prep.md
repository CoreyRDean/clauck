# Automatic standup preparation

**Who:** Developer on a team with daily standups

**Intent:** "30 minutes before my standup, I want a brief of what I did yesterday (from git commits, merged PRs, and completed Jira tickets) and what's on my plate today (open tickets, PRs needing review, today's calendar). I want this ready so I can glance at it and speak confidently without scrambling."

**Context:** Standup prep is a 5-minute scramble: opening GitHub, Jira, and calendar, trying to remember what you worked on yesterday. The information exists across tools; the user just needs it compiled. The standup itself is 2 minutes — the prep shouldn't take longer than the meeting.

**Success:** A brief appears 30 minutes before each standup (detected via Google Calendar). The user reads it in 1 minute. It's accurate enough that they don't need to cross-reference any tool. Format is bullet-friendly — easy to read aloud.
