# "Remind me to..." that actually does the thing

**Who:** Anyone who sets reminders but ignores them

**Intent:** "When I say 'remind me to review the Q3 budget on Thursday', I don't want a notification that says 'review the Q3 budget.' I want the budget opened, a summary of changes since I last looked at it, and a draft of my comments — ready for me to engage with."

**Context:** Traditional reminders are just alarms. The user still has to do all the work when the reminder fires. The value of an agent-powered reminder is that it can pre-do the preparation, so the reminder delivers a ready-to-act artifact, not just a nudge.

**Success:** The user says "remind me to [do X] on [date/time]." A one-shot job fires at that time, does the preparation work, and delivers an artifact the user can immediately engage with — not a notification they have to act on from scratch.
